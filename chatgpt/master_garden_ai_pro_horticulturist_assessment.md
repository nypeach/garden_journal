## üåø Expert Assessment ‚Äì Section Topics

### 2. **Nursery Questions & Expressed Concerns**
- Specific questions asked by the nursery
- Any stated worries, suspicions, or uncertainties (e.g., disease, overwatering, decline)
- Concerns documented verbatim to preserve intent and context
- No conclusions drawn in this section

---

### 3. **Current Plant Status Overview**
- Quick professional ‚Äúread‚Äù of the plant at a glance
- Overall vigor, posture, color, and growth momentum
- Whether the plant looks stable, improving, or declining *since yesterday*

---

### 4. **Growth Stage & Phenological Phase**
- Vegetative, flowering, fruiting, dormancy, recovery, etc.
- Whether current care aligns with this stage
- Expected physiological priorities at this phase (root vs canopy vs reproductive growth)

---

### 5. **Canopy & Structural Assessment (Visual)**
- Leaf color, turgor, gloss, thickness
- New growth quality vs older tissue condition
- Any deformities, burn, chlorosis, spotting, mechanical damage
- Canopy density and airflow implications

---

### 6. **Root Zone & Substrate Condition**
- Soil/media structure and aeration
- Drainage behavior inferred from probe + history
- Compaction, hydrophobicity, salt accumulation risk
- Root-zone temperature considerations (if relevant)

---

### 7. **Probe Readings Interpretation**
- Moisture status **relative to time of day**
- pH suitability for the species *right now*
- EC / fertilization level trend (rising, stable, depleting)
- Alignment or mismatch between probe data and visual cues
- Confidence level in the readings (e.g., fresh vs lagging signals)

---

### 8. **Water Status & Transpiration Balance**
- Whether the plant is currently:
  - Actively transpiring
  - Holding moisture
  - Under mild or acute water stress
- Interaction with today‚Äôs temperature, humidity, and wind
- Risk of over- or under-watering if action is taken now

---

### 9. **Nutrient Status & Feeding Readiness**
- Signs of nutrient sufficiency or deficiency
- Whether the plant is physiologically ready to uptake nutrients
- Timing considerations (morning vs afternoon feeding)
- Risk of salt stress or lockout

---

### 10. **pH & Nutrient Availability Context**
- How current pH affects nutrient uptake *today*
- Whether correction is urgent, optional, or unnecessary
- Short-term vs long-term pH strategy

---

### 11. **Pest & Disease Surveillance**
- Visible indicators from photos or inspection
- Environmental conditions favoring pests/pathogens (humidity, warmth, rainfall)
- Preventative vs reactive posture
- Whether action is warranted now or should be monitored

---

### 12. **Weather Impact Analysis**
- How **today‚Äôs** conditions affect immediate decisions
- Tonight‚Äôs risks (cold stress, saturation, fungal pressure)
- Tomorrow‚Äôs forecast implications for:
  - Watering
  - Feeding
  - Protection or staging
- Cumulative stress from recent weather trends

---

### 13. **Timing & Circadian Considerations**
- Whether actions are optimal *at this hour*
- Plant metabolic rhythm alignment (morning vs afternoon vs evening)
- Risks of delaying vs acting immediately

---

### 14. **Focused Concern Analysis (If Applicable)**
- Direct evaluation of any nursery-expressed concern
- Whether available evidence supports or contradicts the concern
- Biological plausibility based on probe data, visuals, weather, and history
- Risk level assessment (low / moderate / high)
- Clear explanation to resolve uncertainty

---

### 15. **Immediate Action Recommendations**
- Clear, prioritized actions (or confirmation of no action)
- Watering, feeding, pH adjustment, treatment, or restraint
- Quantity, method, and timing guidance
- What **not** to do today (often just as important)

---

### 16. **Responses to Nursery Questions**
- Direct answers to previously stated nursery questions
- References to findings from the expert assessment
- Clear, practical explanations without unnecessary jargon
- Confirmation or correction of assumptions
